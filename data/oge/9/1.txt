The Victoria Falls is one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World. It is the longest waterfall on Earth and is located in South Africa. Its height is about 108 meters. The water makes a loud noise as it falls over the cliff and down into the river below. David Livingstone was the first European who saw the waters of the fall in 1855. He was greatly impressed and named the falls Victoria in honour of the British Queen. Nowadays, tourists from all over the world visit Victoria Falls National Park.
